Arrhenius equation & temperature

The higher the temperature, the more molecules sustain energy required to overcome the activation barrier. Therefore, the rate constant increases exponentially with temperature. In a Haber-Bosch process, high temperature is correlated with higher success, with a tradeoff in equilibrium yield.

↑P → ↑[conc]

Pressure & collision frequency

Higher pressure results in higher compression of gas molecules into a smaller volume, resulting in an increased concentration and effective collisions per unit time. Le Chatelier's principle suggests that higher pressure shifts the equilibrium towards lower pressure. Forwards produces 2 moles of NH₃, reverse produces 4 moles of reactants (N₂ + 3H₂). Therefore, high pressure shifts the equilibrium towards the forward direction (NH₃).

Ea ↓ → rate ↑

Iron catalyst & activation energy

The iron catalyst lowers the activation energy by allowing the reaction to proceed through an alternative reaction pathway. The alternative pathway enables molecules to require less energy to successfully overcome the activation barrier, resulting in the reaction taking place at a reasonable temperature. In contrast, reaction without an iron catalyst requires extreme temperatures, making it economically challenging.

⇌ balance

The rate-yield tradeoff

High temperature increases the rate of a reaction, however they also shift the equilibrium away from NH₃ production, due to the fact that the reaction is exothermic, negatively impacting the product yield. Using low temperature would give increased yield, but is unviable as the reaction would proceed too slowly. A temperature of 450 degrees Celsius with a 150-300atm pressure range, and an iron catalyst, is a solution to best overcome this tradeoff.

Carl Bosch & Fritz Haber

Haber was a German chemist responsible for developing the synthesis of ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen. Bosch was a chemical engineer who spent his life tackling the challenge of scaling Haber's synthesis reaction because of the high pressures involved.

Both received Nobel prizes for their work, and their partnership allowed chemistry to be applied on an industrial scale.

Significance

Their discoveries were essential for resolving the shortage of the world's food supply. By producing NH₃ on an industrial scale, nitrate fertilizer can be produced, which greatly increases the growth of crops.

1.6 billion (1900) to 8 billion crops.

Controversy

Haber's credibility is complicated as he was also responsible for developing chlorine gas used in World War 1, resulting in thousands of deaths in his name. His own wife committed suicide in protest against his work.

Furthermore, the synthesis of ammonia has environmental impacts, such as fertilizer runoff causing algal blooms resulting in dead zones in waterways, and the production of CO₂ contributing to global warming.

Modern Research

Reinvention of the synthesis of ammonia on a global industrial scale is in effect. Researchers are focusing on the use of renewable electricity to drive nitrogen fixation to produce ammonia. Researchers are explaining the use of nitrogenase enzymes to fix N₂ at room temperature and pressure.

Production of fuel cells based on ammonia is also being developed. Overall, Haber-Bosch chemistry has provided a foundation not only for agriculture but also for the future of clean energy.
